# IRS Form 1040 Instructions — How to Complete Your Federal Income Tax Return

*Source: IRS Form 1040 Instructions (Tax Year 2024). For authoritative guidance, visit irs.gov.*

---

## Overview of Form 1040

Form 1040 is the standard US individual income tax return. For tax year 2024, the return is due by April 15, 2025. If April 15 falls on a weekend or holiday, the deadline moves to the next business day.

The form is two pages and is supplemented by additional schedules for more complex tax situations:
- **Schedule 1:** Additional Income and Adjustments
- **Schedule 2:** Additional Taxes
- **Schedule 3:** Additional Credits and Payments
- **Schedule A:** Itemized Deductions
- **Schedule B:** Interest and Ordinary Dividends
- **Schedule C:** Profit or Loss from Business (Sole Proprietorship)
- **Schedule D:** Capital Gains and Losses
- **Schedule E:** Supplemental Income and Loss (rental, S-corps, partnerships)
- **Schedule SE:** Self-Employment Tax

---

## Part I: Filing Information

### Name and Address
Enter your legal name exactly as it appears on your Social Security card. If married filing jointly, enter both spouses' names.

### Social Security Number (SSN)
Your SSN is required. If you don't have a SSN, use your Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN). ITINs are issued by the IRS to individuals who are not eligible for a SSN but have a US tax filing obligation.

### Filing Status (Lines 1–5)
Check only one filing status box. Your filing status determines your standard deduction amount and which tax rates apply to your taxable income. See the Filing Status section in the Publication 17 knowledge document for definitions.

### Digital Assets (Line 1)
You must answer "Yes" or "No" to the question about digital assets (cryptocurrency, NFTs, etc.). Answer "Yes" if at any time during 2024 you received, sold, exchanged, transferred, or otherwise disposed of digital assets or had a financial interest in any digital asset.

---

## Part II: Income

### Line 1a: Total Wages, Salaries, Tips, etc.
Enter total wages from all Forms W-2, Box 1. If you received tips not shown on your W-2, include them here.

### Line 1b–1h: Other Compensation
Several lines capture additional compensation not in Box 1 of your W-2, including:
- Household employee wages (paid without W-2)
- Tip income not reported to employer
- Medicaid waiver payments
- Taxable dependent care benefits

### Line 2b: Taxable Interest
Total taxable interest income from Form 1099-INT and similar sources. If more than $1,500, you must also complete Schedule B.

### Line 3b: Ordinary Dividends
Total ordinary dividends from Form 1099-DIV. If more than $1,500, also complete Schedule B.

### Line 3a: Qualified Dividends
The portion of Line 3b that qualifies for the lower long-term capital gains tax rates. Reported in Box 1b of Form 1099-DIV.

### Line 4a/4b: IRA Distributions
Line 4a is the total distribution; 4b is the taxable amount. If the entire distribution is taxable (common for traditional IRAs), these amounts are the same.

### Line 5a/5b: Pensions and Annuities
Line 5a is the total; 5b is the taxable amount. The taxable portion depends on whether contributions were pre-tax or after-tax (simplified method calculation).

### Line 6a/6b: Social Security Benefits
Line 6a is total Social Security benefits (from SSA-1099); 6b is the taxable amount (0%, 50%, or 85% depending on your combined income — see Publication 17 knowledge document).

### Line 7: Capital Gain or (Loss)
Net capital gain or loss from Schedule D. If the net is a loss, enter as a negative (up to $3,000 limit, or $1,500 for MFS).

### Line 8: Additional Income from Schedule 1
Transfers total additional income from Schedule 1, Line 10, including business income (Schedule C), rental income (Schedule E), alimony received (pre-2019), and other income.

### Line 9: Total Income
Sum of Lines 1z through 8. This is your gross income before any adjustments.

---

## Part III: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

### Line 10: Adjustments from Schedule 1
Transfers adjustments from Schedule 1, Part II, including student loan interest deduction, IRA deduction, self-employed health insurance deduction, alimony paid, etc.

### Line 11: Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)
Line 9 minus Line 10. This is the key figure used to determine eligibility for many deductions and credits. Lower AGI = broader eligibility.

---

## Part IV: Standard or Itemized Deduction

### Line 12: Standard Deduction or Itemized Deductions
You may take either the standard deduction or your total itemized deductions (from Schedule A), whichever is higher. Most taxpayers take the standard deduction because the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 nearly doubled it.

**Standard deduction amounts for 2024:**
- Single: $14,600
- MFJ/Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $29,200
- MFS: $14,600
- Head of Household: $21,900

Check the "Someone can claim you as a dependent" box if applicable — this significantly reduces your standard deduction.

### Line 13: Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction
Self-employed individuals and owners of pass-through businesses (partnerships, S-corps, sole proprietorships) may deduct up to 20% of qualified business income. Subject to complex limitations at higher income levels. (Form 8995 or 8995-A)

### Line 15: Taxable Income
Line 11 minus Lines 12 and 13. This is the income on which your federal tax is calculated.

---

## Part V: Tax Calculation

### Line 16: Tax
The tax on your taxable income from the 2024 Tax Tables or Tax Computation Worksheet. The 2024 federal income tax brackets (ordinary income):

| Rate | Single | MFJ |
|---|---|---|
| 10% | $0–$11,600 | $0–$23,200 |
| 12% | $11,601–$47,150 | $23,201–$94,300 |
| 22% | $47,151–$100,525 | $94,301–$201,050 |
| 24% | $100,526–$191,950 | $201,051–$383,900 |
| 32% | $191,951–$243,725 | $383,901–$487,450 |
| 35% | $243,726–$609,350 | $487,451–$731,200 |
| 37% | Over $609,350 | Over $731,200 |

These are marginal rates — only income in each bracket is taxed at that rate.

### Line 17: Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)
Calculated on Form 6251. The AMT is a parallel tax system designed to ensure high-income taxpayers pay a minimum amount. The 2024 AMT exemption is $85,700 (single) and $133,300 (MFJ). Most middle-income taxpayers are not subject to AMT.

### Lines 19–24: Non-Refundable Credits
Credits that reduce your tax but not below zero:
- Child tax credit / credit for other dependents (Line 19)
- Education credits (Line 20, Form 8863)
- Retirement savings contributions credit (Saver's Credit, Line 20)
- Residential clean energy credit (Line 20)
- Credit for child and dependent care expenses (Line 31, Schedule 3)

### Line 24: Total Tax
Sum of all tax and additional taxes, reduced by non-refundable credits.

---

## Part VI: Payments

### Line 25: Federal Income Tax Withheld
Sum of all federal income tax withheld, shown in Box 2 of Form W-2 and Box 4 of Form 1099 documents.

### Line 26: Estimated Tax Payments
Payments made during the year using Form 1040-ES. If you are self-employed or have income not subject to withholding, you are generally required to make quarterly estimated payments.

**2024 Estimated Tax Payment Due Dates:**
- Q1: April 15, 2024
- Q2: June 17, 2024
- Q3: September 16, 2024
- Q4: January 15, 2025

### Line 27: Earned Income Credit (EIC)
Refundable credit. Schedule EIC must be attached if you have qualifying children.

### Line 28: Additional Child Tax Credit (ACTC)
The refundable portion of the Child Tax Credit (Form 8812).

### Line 29: American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)
40% of the AOTC (up to $1,000) is refundable (Form 8863).

### Line 33: Total Payments
Sum of all taxes withheld, estimated payments, and refundable credits.

---

## Part VII: Refund or Amount Owed

### Line 35a: Refund Amount
If Line 33 (total payments) exceeds Line 24 (total tax), you have an overpayment. The amount on Line 35a is your refund. You can direct deposit to up to three accounts.

### Line 37: Amount You Owe
If your total tax exceeds your total payments, you owe the difference. Payment is due by April 15, 2025, regardless of whether you filed an extension.

**Underpayment Penalty:** If you owe $1,000 or more in tax after subtracting withholding and estimated payments, you may owe an underpayment penalty (Form 2210). Safe harbors: pay at least 90% of current-year tax, or 100% of prior-year tax (110% if prior-year AGI exceeded $150,000).

---

## Filing Extensions

If you cannot file by April 15, you can get an automatic 6-month extension to October 15, 2025 by filing Form 4868. An extension to file is NOT an extension to pay — you must still pay any taxes owed by April 15 to avoid penalties and interest.

**Failure-to-File Penalty:** 5% of unpaid taxes per month (up to 25%).
**Failure-to-Pay Penalty:** 0.5% of unpaid taxes per month (up to 25%).
**Interest:** Currently charged at the federal short-term rate + 3% (compounded daily).

---

## Amended Returns

To correct a filed return, use Form 1040-X. You generally have 3 years from the original due date to file an amended return and claim a refund. Amended returns can be filed electronically or by mail. Expect processing to take 16–20 weeks for paper-filed amendments.
